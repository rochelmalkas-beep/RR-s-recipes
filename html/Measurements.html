RR's Kitchen Measuring Conversions

Converting kitchen measurements is crucial for multiplying or cutting a recipe down. Use the tables or the converter on the right to quickly convert between units.

Liquid Measurement Conversions

Use the following liquid measurement conversions to easily adjust your recipes.

1 fluid ounce = 2 tablespoons
1 cup = 8 fluid ounces
1 pint = 2 cups = 16 fluid ounces
1 quart = 2 pints = 32 fluid ounces
1 gallon = 4 quarts = 128 fluid ounces

Dry Measurement Conversions

Use the following dry measurement conversions to easily adjust your recipes.

1 tablespoon = 3 teaspoons
1/4 cup = 4 tablespoons
1/3 cup = 5 tablespoons + 1 teaspoon
1/2 cup = 8 tablespoons
1 cup = 16 tablespoons

Order of Measuring Cups & Spoons

Standard measuring cup sizes (smallest to largest): 1/8 cup, 1/4 cup, 1/3 cup, 1/2 cup, 1 cup.

Standard measuring spoon sizes (smallest to largest): 1/4 teaspoon, 1/2 teaspoon, 1 teaspoon, 1/2 tablespoon, 1 tablespoon.

Liquid Measuring Cups vs Dry

Liquid measuring cups are for liquids (water, milk, oil) and have graduated markings. Dry measuring cups are for dry ingredients (flour, sugar) and are designed to be level-filled. Note: For many dry ingredients a weight measurement (grams) is the most accurate.

How to Measure Liquid Ingredients

  1. Use a liquid measuring cup placed on a level surface.
  2. Position your eyes level with the markings.
  3. Pour until the liquid is level with the desired line.

How to Measure Dry Ingredients

  1. Use a dry ingredient measuring cup for larger quantities and a measuring spoon for small ones.
  2. Level the ingredient with a flat edge (spatula or knife).
  3. Pack moist ingredients (like brown sugar) tightly into the cup.
